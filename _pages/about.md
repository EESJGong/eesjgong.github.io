I am an M.S. student in Information and Communication Engineering at the School of Electronic and Information Engineering, South China University of Technology, advised by Prof. Shuangping Huang.

My research so far has centered on **3D generative modeling** and **LLM-driven structured generation**, spanning **digital humans**, **neural rendering**, and **text-guided 3D/CAD synthesis**. Through these projects, I became increasingly interested in a broader question: how generative systems can incorporate **physical grounding**, **environment dynamics prediction**, and **self-improving capability**. This has led me to my current interests in **LLM agents**, **world models**, and **physically grounded 3D generation**.

More specifically, I am interested in building generative systems that are not only expressive, but also **structured, controllable, and executable**. My recent work explores long-horizon **Text-to-CAD** generation, where natural language instructions are translated into geometric decomposition graphs, CAD action sequences, and executable `bpy` code. I am particularly interested in integrating **geometric representations**, **multimodal reasoning**, and **agentic generation frameworks** to enable more reliable and physically meaningful 3D content creation.

Previously, I also worked on **speech-driven 3D facial animation** and **Gaussian-based talking head generation**, with publications at **ECCV 2024** and **CVPR 2025**. More recently, my work on **hierarchical and geometry-aware graph representations for Text-to-CAD** was accepted to **ICLR 2026**.

I am broadly interested in the intersection of **foundation models**, **3D/embodied generation**, and **structured decision-making systems**. My long-term goal is to develop intelligent generative systems that can reason over structure, interact with tools and environments, and continually improve through feedback.

Please feel free to reach out for academic discussion or potential collaboration.
